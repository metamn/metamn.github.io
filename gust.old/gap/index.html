Gap

Older people are living in the past and in the future. They are driven by emotions and feelings, they think with the heart and they do a lot.

Young people are living in the present. They have no human feelings but most likely animal instincts. They think with the brain, if they think.

The culture of fear — past and future driving present — was replaced by the culture of egoism.

If I’m ok right now everybody else is, must and should be ok around.

Old people are afraid of feeling good. They do the drama visibly. New people are always feeling good by wearing a mask and killing their feelings behind.

That’s why there were so many suicides and schizos. Killing feelings sometimes kills the body and temporary the mind.

This is an era when we leave behind old values like love, loneliness, empathy, care. And we replace with a binary mood: either we cry or we laugh. And do these often, many times a day.

Speed kills. We interact so fast, we are so restless we can’t really feel what is happening to us.
