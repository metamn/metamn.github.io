responsiveness

responsiveness

despite working with Smuff since 2006 the fully responsive version of their website come alive in 2013.

the previous version rolled out in 2010 was fully mobile compatible but not responsive. The look was similar but the content was less on mobiles than desktops.

since Smuff had no mobile app yet we've designed the website first for mobile then enhanced up to bigger displays.

content discipline

content discipline

to make the website perfectly aligned we've employed content discipline.

product thumbnails, title and short descriptions have exactly defined dimensions. This way the product grid looks uniform on all devices.

better the content more awesome the design.

selling images

selling images

we've realized early we are selling first of all images. In plus, according to Google Analytics product pages take 70% of all visits.

these findings combined together made us to design a product page which displays the biggest product images possible together with thumbnail navigation to present the product from different point of views.

focus points

focus points

visitors can focus only on five things in a single page. we've implemented these focus points to drive visitors attention to the right action and to keep them on site.

you can understand better how we increased conversion with Smuff in a short case study.

single page, single click checkout

single page, single click checkout

we've put major focus on designing the checkout page. Studies show visitors drop shopping most likely at this last stage.

we've managed to create a single page checkout where returning users are one click away from purchase.

new users have just a single additional step, the registration with a click on "Connect with Facebeook" or filling out a short form without leaving the checkout screen.
