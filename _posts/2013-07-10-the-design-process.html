An Approach To Web Design

This is a case study to present an effective approach to web design using industry best practices.

TLDR;

  1. Design around the message
  2. One screen at once
  3. Five elements on a screen
  4. Uniform design elements
  5. Industry standards

1. Design around the message

Every website sells something. A book, a blog post, a subscription, a photo — an information.

The first step is to identify what the website sells, how this item feels, and how people buying them will feel about.

In our case Smuff sells physical products. Represented by photos, short and long descriptions and price. So we are in the business of selling photos, first of all.

Smuff products are high-tech, bleeding edge, unique, never seen before toys and useful gifts. Which make visitors feel special, lucky to have the opportunity to meet, see and know them.

For sure when they decide to buy they'll feel happy — finally a good, outstanding, stylish gift is found and can be given to anybody with guaranteed success and satisfaction.

So what's the main design element driving the entire site and what message displays?

  1. Is it something special?
  2. Does it make you happy?
  3. Is something techy / geeky going on here?

I hope identifying and designing the most important element of the page is done. And people will find it cool, powerful and happy.

Notes

  • When designing Smuff the first element done was the product thumb, then the main menu, then the logo.
  • This made us clear the design elements we will re-use will be borders and backgrounds.
  • And this striped background won't be reused ever. It is so strong it will serve only the main design element, the product thumb.
